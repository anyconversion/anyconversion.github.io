Gradians to Degrees Converter

Definition

A gradian (grad or gon) is a unit of angular measure where a full circle is 400 gradians, making a right angle 100 gradians, used in some surveying and engineering fields. A degree (째) divides a circle into 360 equal parts, the most common angular unit globally.

History

Gradians were introduced during the French Revolution in the 1790s as part of the metric system, aiming for a decimal-based angle unit (400 grads = 360째), though they never gained widespread use. Degrees date back to Babylonian astronomy around 2000 BCE, standardized through Greek and medieval European mathematics.
