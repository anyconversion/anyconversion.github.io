Radians to Degrees Converter

Definition

A radian (rad) is a unit of angular measure defined as the angle subtended by an arc equal in length to the radius of a circle, where 2π radians equals 360°. A degree (°) is a unit of angular measure dividing a circle into 360 equal parts, widely used in everyday and technical contexts.

History

Radians were introduced in the 18th century by mathematicians like Roger Cotes, formalized by James Thomson in 1873, tied to the natural properties of circles in calculus. Degrees trace back to ancient Babylonians around 2000 BCE, who used a base-60 system, later refined by Greek astronomers like Hipparchus.
