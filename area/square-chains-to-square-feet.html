Square Chains to Square Feet Converter

Definition

Square chain (ch²) is an imperial unit of area equal to the area of a square with sides of 1 chain (66 feet). One square chain equals 4,356 square feet. The square foot (ft²) is defined as the area of a square with sides of 1 foot, commonly used in the United States and other countries that use the imperial system.

History

The chain was introduced by Edmund Gunter in the 17th century as a surveying tool, with the square chain becoming useful for land measurement. It was particularly valuable because an area of 10 square chains equals one acre, making calculations easier. While the square foot remains in common use, the square chain is now primarily used in specialized land surveying and legal land descriptions.
