Degrees to Radians Converter

Definition

A degree (°) is a unit of angular measure where a full circle is divided into 360 equal parts, commonly used in navigation and engineering. A radian (rad) measures an angle where the arc length equals the radius, with 360° equaling 2π radians, standard in mathematics and physics.

History

Degrees originated with the Babylonians around 2000 BCE, using a sexagesimal (base-60) system, adopted by Greek astronomers like Ptolemy. Radians were conceptualized in the 18th century and named in 1873 by James Thomson, reflecting their utility in calculus and circular geometry.
