Definition

A cup is a unit of volume in the US customary system, commonly used in cooking, equal to 8 fluid ounces or approximately 236.58824 milliliters. A fluid ounce (fl oz) is a unit of volume, distinct from the weight ounce, equal to 1/8 of a cup or about 29.57353 milliliters in the US. These units are standardized for liquid ingredients in recipes.

History

The cup emerged in the 19th century in the US as cookbooks standardized measurements, replacing vague terms like "handful." It was tied to the fluid ounce, which traces back to British imperial measures. The fluid ounce itself evolved from the wine gallon, a medieval English unit, and was adapted in the US with a slightly different volume than its imperial counterpart (28.413 mL).
