Dashes to Milliliters Converter

Definition

A dash is an imprecise unit of volume in cooking and bartending, typically a small amount of liquid (like bitters or vinegar) shaken from a bottle, roughly 1/8 of a teaspoon or about 0.616115 milliliters. A milliliter (mL) is a metric unit of volume, equal to 1/1000 of a liter, widely used for precise liquid measurements.

History

The dash originated in 19th-century mixology, tied to the rise of cocktails, where small flavor additions were needed without exact tools. It remains a subjective measure, varying by container and technique. The milliliter, part of the metric system established in the 1790s, gained traction in cooking as kitchen scales and graduated tools became common in the 20th century.
