Bytes to Kilobytes Converter

Definition

A byte (B) is a unit of digital storage equal to 8 bits, typically representing one character of data, such as a letter or number. A kilobyte (KB) is 1,000 bytes in decimal terms, commonly used to measure small files or memory sizes in computing.

History

The byte was coined in 1956 by Werner Buchholz at IBM, standardizing 8 bits to simplify computer architecture over earlier variable-length units. The kilobyte emerged in the 1960s as digital storage grew, adopting the metric prefix “kilo” (1000) for convenience, though early binary usage (1024) caused ongoing confusion.
