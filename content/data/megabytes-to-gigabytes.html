Megabytes to Gigabytes Converter

Definition

A megabyte (MB) is a unit of digital storage equal to 1,000 kilobytes or 1,000,000 bytes in decimal terms, used for medium-sized files like images or documents. A gigabyte (GB) is 1,000 megabytes or 1,000,000,000 bytes, commonly used for larger data such as videos or software.

History

The megabyte was introduced in the 1970s as storage devices expanded beyond kilobytes, leveraging the metric prefix “mega” (10⁶). The gigabyte followed in the 1980s with the advent of hard drives and larger memory needs, standardized in decimal form by manufacturers despite binary (2³⁰) usage in some technical contexts.
