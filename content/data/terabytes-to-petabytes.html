Terabytes to Petabytes Converter

Definition

A terabyte (TB) is a unit of digital storage equal to 1,000 gigabytes or 10¹² bytes, used for large datasets like backups or media libraries. A petabyte (PB) is 1,000 terabytes or 10¹⁵ bytes, applied to massive storage systems such as data centers or cloud archives.

History

The terabyte emerged in the 1990s as personal computing and enterprise storage scaled up, named with the metric prefix “tera” (10¹²). The petabyte followed in the 2000s with the rise of big data and internet infrastructure, coined from “peta” (10¹⁵), reflecting exponential growth in digital storage needs.
