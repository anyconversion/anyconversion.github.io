Electronvolts to Joules Converter

Definition

An electronvolt (eV) is a unit of energy equal to the energy gained by an electron accelerated through a potential difference of 1 volt, approximately 1.602176634 × 10⁻¹⁹ joules, used in particle physics. A joule (J) is the SI unit, 1 newton-meter.

History

The electronvolt was introduced in the early 20th century with the development of quantum mechanics, formalized in 1913 by Robert Millikan’s oil-drop experiment, ideal for atomic-scale energy. The joule, named after James Prescott Joule, was defined in 1882 and adopted by SI in 1960.
