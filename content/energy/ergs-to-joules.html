Ergs to Joules Converter

Definition

An erg (erg) is a unit of energy in the CGS system, equal to the work done by a force of 1 dyne over 1 centimeter, or 10⁻⁷ joules, used in early physics. A joule (J) is the SI unit of energy, equal to 1 newton-meter or 10⁷ ergs.

History

The erg was introduced in 1865 by the British Association for the Advancement of Science as part of the CGS system, suited for small-scale measurements. The joule, named after James Prescott Joule, was defined in 1882 and adopted by the SI system in 1960, replacing ergs.
