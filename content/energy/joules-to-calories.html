Joules to Calories Converter

Definition

A joule (J) is the SI unit of energy, equal to the work done by a force of one newton over one meter (1 J = 1 N·m), used in physics and engineering. A calorie (cal) is a unit of energy defined as the amount needed to raise 1 gram of water by 1°C, equal to approximately 4.184 joules.

History

The joule was named after James Prescott Joule in 1882, adopted by the SI system in 1960 for his work on heat and energy equivalence. The calorie emerged in the 1820s from French physicist Nicolas Clément, used in thermodynamics and nutrition, standardized in the 19th century.
