Dyne to Newtons Converter

Definition

A dyne (dyn) is a unit of force in the CGS system, defined as the force required to accelerate a 1-gram mass by 1 centimeter per second squared (1 dyn = 1 g·cm/s²), equal to 10⁻⁵ newtons. A newton (N) is the SI unit, equal to 1 kg·m/s², or 100,000 dynes.

History

The dyne was introduced in 1873 as part of the centimeter-gram-second (CGS) system by the British Association for the Advancement of Science, suited for smaller-scale physics. The newton, honoring Isaac Newton, was established in 1948 with the SI system, replacing CGS units like dyne in modern science.
