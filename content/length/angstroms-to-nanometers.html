Angstroms to Nanometers Converter

Definition

An angstrom (Å) is a unit of length equal to 10^-10 meters (one ten-billionth of a meter). A nanometer (nm) is a unit of length equal to 10^-9 meters (one billionth of a meter). One nanometer equals 10 angstroms.

History

The angstrom was named after Swedish physicist Anders Jonas Ångström (1814-1874), who used it for measuring the wavelengths of light. It became widely used in spectrography, atomic physics, and crystallography. While not officially part of the International System of Units (SI), the angstrom remains popular in fields like chemistry, physics, and crystallography for describing atomic-scale dimensions, bond lengths, and wavelengths. The nanometer, officially adopted into the SI system when the prefix "nano-" was standardized in 1960, has largely replaced the angstrom in many scientific contexts, though both units continue to be used depending on the field and application.
