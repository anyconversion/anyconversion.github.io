Meters to Yards Converter

Definition

A meter is the base unit of length in the International System of Units (SI), defined as the length of the path traveled by light in a vacuum in 1/299,792,458 of a second. A yard is a unit of length in the imperial and US customary systems, defined as 3 feet or exactly 0.9144 meters. One meter equals approximately 1.0936 yards.

History

The meter was introduced by the French in 1793 during the French Revolution as part of the metric system, originally defined as one ten-millionth of the distance from the equator to the North Pole. The yard has origins in medieval England, where it was standardized by the 13th century. There are several theories about its origin, including that it represented the length from the tip of King Henry I's nose to the end of his outstretched thumb. The current international definition of the yard as exactly 0.9144 meters was established in 1959 by agreement between the United States and countries of the Commonwealth.
