Cheval-Vapeur to Horsepower Converter

Definition

Cheval-vapeur (CV), or metric horsepower, is a unit of power equal to 735.49875 watts, defined as the power to lift 75 kg by 1 meter in 1 second, used in Europe. Horsepower (hp), mechanical horsepower, is approximately 745.699871582 watts, standard in the US and UK.

History

Cheval-vapeur was established in France in the 19th century as a metric alternative to British horsepower, formalized for engine ratings in continental Europe. Horsepower was created by James Watt in the 1780s to compare steam engines to horses, set at 33,000 foot-pounds per minute, and adopted widely in English-speaking countries.
