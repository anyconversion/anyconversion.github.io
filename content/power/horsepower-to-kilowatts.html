Horsepower to Kilowatts Converter

Definition

Horsepower (hp) is a unit of power, where 1 mechanical horsepower equals about 745.699871582 watts, commonly used for engines in the US and UK. A kilowatt (kW) is a metric unit of power equal to 1,000 watts or 1,000 joules per second, standard in scientific and electrical contexts.

History

Horsepower was invented by James Watt in the 1780s to market steam engines, defined as 33,000 foot-pounds per minute, rooted in industrial revolution needs. The kilowatt emerged with the metric system in the 19th century, named after Watt, and was formalized in 1960 by the SI system for global use.
