Watts to Horsepower Converter

Definition

A watt (W) is the SI unit of power, equal to one joule per second (1 W = 1 J/s), measuring the rate of energy transfer or work done. Horsepower (hp) is a unit of power, typically mechanical horsepower, where 1 hp equals approximately 745.699871582 watts, used to rate engines and motors.

History

The watt was named after James Watt in 1882, adopted by the SI system in 1960 to standardize power measurements, reflecting his steam engine improvements. Horsepower was devised by Watt in the late 18th century, comparing engine output to the work of draft horses, standardized at 33,000 foot-pounds per minute.
