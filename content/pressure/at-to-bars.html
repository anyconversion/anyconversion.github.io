Atmosphere (Technical) to Bars Converter

Definition

An atmosphere technical (at) is a unit of pressure equal to the force exerted by a 1-kilogram mass per square centimeter under standard gravity, approximately 98,066.5 pascals or 0.980665 bars. A bar is a metric unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals, close to standard atmospheric pressure.

History

The technical atmosphere was introduced in the early 20th century in engineering, particularly in Germany, as a practical unit tied to kilogram-force (1 at = 1 kgf/cmÂ²), used in hydraulics and pneumatics. The bar, proposed by Napier Shaw in 1909, became a metric standard for its near equivalence to atmospheric pressure, adopted widely in industry.
