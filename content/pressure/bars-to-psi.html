Bars to PSI Converter

Definition

A bar is a metric unit of pressure equal to 100,000 pascals, roughly the atmospheric pressure at sea level (1 bar ≈ 0.986923 atm). Pounds per square inch (psi) is a US customary unit of pressure, defined as the force of one pound-force over one square inch, equal to about 6,894.75729 pascals.

History

The bar was introduced in 1909 by British meteorologist Napier Shaw, derived from “barye” in the CGS system, and gained use in engineering for its near-atmospheric value. PSI emerged in the 19th century in the US, tied to industrial needs like steam engines, standardized in American engineering practices.
