Inches of Mercury to Pascals Converter

Definition

Inches of mercury (inHg) is a unit of pressure based on the height of a mercury column in a barometer, where 1 inHg equals approximately 3,386.389 pascals at standard gravity and temperature. A pascal (Pa) is the SI unit of pressure, equal to 1 newton per square meter (1 Pa = 1 N/m²).

History

Inches of mercury originated with Torricelli’s 1643 barometer, adapted in the 18th century to English units, standardized at 29.92 inHg for 1 atm by the 19th century in aviation and meteorology. The pascal, named after Blaise Pascal, was adopted in 1971 by the SI system, unifying pressure measurements in science.
