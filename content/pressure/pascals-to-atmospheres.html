Pascals to Atmospheres Converter

Definition

A pascal (Pa) is the SI unit of pressure, defined as one newton per square meter (1 Pa = 1 N/m²), used in science and engineering. An atmosphere (atm) is a unit of pressure equal to the average air pressure at sea level, approximately 101,325 pascals, often used in meteorology and diving.

History

The pascal was named after Blaise Pascal in 1971, adopted by the SI system to standardize pressure measurements, reflecting his work on fluid mechanics. The atmosphere was defined in the 17th century by Evangelista Torricelli’s barometer experiments, standardized in 1927 as 101,325 Pa for practical use.
