Gauss to Teslas Converter

Definition

A gauss (G) is a unit of magnetic flux density in the CGS system, equal to 1 maxwell per square centimeter, or 10⁻⁴ teslas. A tesla (T) is the SI unit of magnetic flux density, equal to 1 weber per square meter, used to measure magnetic fields in modern science.

History

The gauss was named after Carl Friedrich Gauss in the 19th century, reflecting his contributions to magnetism, and became standard in the CGS system. The tesla, named after Nikola Tesla, was adopted in 1960 by the SI system, replacing older units like gauss for its alignment with metric standards.
