Poise to Pascal-Seconds Converter

Definition

A poise (P) is a unit of dynamic viscosity in the CGS system, equal to 1 gram per centimeter-second, or 0.1 pascal-seconds. A pascal-second (Pa·s) is the SI unit of viscosity, measuring resistance to flow, equal to 1 newton-second per square meter.

History

The poise was introduced in the 19th century, named after Jean Léonard Marie Poiseuille, a French physician who studied fluid flow in tubes, formalized in the CGS system. The pascal-second emerged with the SI system in the 1960s, named after Blaise Pascal, integrating viscosity into modern metric standards.
