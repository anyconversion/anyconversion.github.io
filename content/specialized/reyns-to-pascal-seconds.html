Reyns to Pascal-Seconds Converter

Definition

A reyn is a unit of dynamic viscosity in the British gravitational system, equal to 1 pound-force-second per square inch, or approximately 6894.75729 pascal-seconds. A pascal-second (Pa·s) is the SI unit of viscosity, equal to 1 newton-second per square meter, used for fluid dynamics.

History

The reyn was named after Osborne Reynolds, a 19th-century engineer known for the Reynolds number in fluid mechanics, introduced in the early 20th century for engineering in the US. The pascal-second, part of the SI system since the 1960s, honors Blaise Pascal and replaced imperial units like the reyn in scientific use.
