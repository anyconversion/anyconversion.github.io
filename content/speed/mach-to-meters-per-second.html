Definition

Mach is a unit of speed relative to the speed of sound, where Mach 1 equals approximately 343 meters per second in dry air at 20Â°C, varying with conditions. Meters per second (m/s) is the SI unit of speed, measuring distance traveled per second.

History

The Mach number was named after Ernst Mach in the 1920s by Jakob Ackeret, introduced for aeronautics as planes approached supersonic speeds in the 20th century. Meters per second was formalized with the metric system in the 1790s, adopted by SI in 1960 for universal scientific use.
