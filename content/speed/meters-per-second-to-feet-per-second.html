Meters per Second to Feet per Second Converter

Definition

Meters per second (m/s) is the SI unit of speed, equal to the distance of 1 meter traveled in 1 second, fundamental in physics. Feet per second (ft/s) is a US customary unit, equal to 1 foot (0.3048 meters) per second, or about 0.3048 meters per second, used in engineering and aviation.

History

Meters per second was established with the metric system in the 1790s, adopted by the SI system in 1960 for scientific consistency. Feet per second emerged in the 17th century in Britain, tied to imperial units, and gained use in the US with industrial and aeronautical developments in the 19th and 20th centuries.
