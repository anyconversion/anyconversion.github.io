Celsius to Fahrenheit (C to F) Converter

Definition

The Celsius scale (°C) is a temperature scale where 0° is the freezing point of water and 100° is its boiling point at standard atmospheric pressure, with each degree equal to 1/100 of that range. The Fahrenheit scale (°F) sets 32° as water’s freezing point and 212° as its boiling point, with 180 degrees between them, commonly used in the US.

History

The Celsius scale was developed in 1742 by Anders Celsius, a Swedish astronomer, originally with 0° as boiling and 100° as freezing, reversed after his death. The Fahrenheit scale was proposed in 1724 by Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit, a German physicist, based on a brine solution’s freezing point and human body temperature, refined over time for practical use.
