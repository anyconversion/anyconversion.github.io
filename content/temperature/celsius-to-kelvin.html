Celsius to Kelvin Converter

Definition

The Celsius scale (°C) measures temperature relative to water’s freezing point at 0° and boiling point at 100°, part of the metric system. The Kelvin scale (K) is an absolute temperature scale where 0 K is absolute zero (-273.15°C), with degrees equal in size to Celsius, used in scientific contexts.

History

Anders Celsius devised the Celsius scale in 1742, later adopted globally with the metric system in the 1790s. The Kelvin scale was proposed by William Thomson (Lord Kelvin) in 1848, building on earlier absolute zero concepts, formalized in the 19th century for thermodynamics and physics research.
