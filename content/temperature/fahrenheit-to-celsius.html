Fahrenheit to Celsius (F to C) Converter

Definition

The Fahrenheit scale (°F) is a temperature scale where 32° marks water’s freezing point and 212° its boiling point, dividing the range into 180 equal degrees. The Celsius scale (°C) assigns 0° to water’s freezing point and 100° to its boiling point, used worldwide in science and most countries.

History

Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit created the Fahrenheit scale in 1724, using a mercury thermometer and reference points like a salt-ice mixture and body temperature, later adjusted for water’s phase changes. Anders Celsius introduced the Celsius scale in 1742, initially inverted, standardized in its current form by the mid-18th century for scientific precision.
