Sidereal Days to Solar Days Converter

Definition

A sidereal day is the time it takes for the Earth to complete one rotation relative to the fixed stars, approximately 23 hours, 56 minutes, and 4.1 seconds (23.9344699 hours). A solar day (mean solar day) is the average time between two consecutive returns of the Sun to the local meridian, exactly 24 hours by definition. One sidereal day equals approximately 0.9972696 solar days.

History

The distinction between sidereal and solar days has been recognized since ancient times. Ancient astronomers in Babylon, Egypt, and Greece observed that the stars rise about 4 minutes earlier each night, reflecting the difference between these two measures of a day. The concept of a sidereal day became more precisely defined with the development of accurate astronomical observations in the 17th and 18th centuries. The modern definition of the solar day as exactly 24 hours was standardized with the adoption of mean solar time in the 19th century, which accounts for the variations in the apparent speed of the Sun throughout the year.
