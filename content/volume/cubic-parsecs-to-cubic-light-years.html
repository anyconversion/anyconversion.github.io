Definition

A cubic parsec (pc続) is a unit of volume equal to a cube with sides measuring one parsec in length. One parsec is approximately 3.26 light-years or 30.9 trillion kilometers. History

The parsec (parallax second) was first defined in 1913 by British astronomer Herbert Hall Turner, derived from the distance at which one astronomical unit subtends an angle of one arcsecond. The light-year was first proposed in 1838 by Friedrich Bessel when measuring the parallax of 61 Cygni. Both units are primarily used in astronomy to express vast cosmic distances and volumes, with cubic versions being utilized to measure the volume of astronomical objects and regions of space.
