Gallons to Liters Converter

Definition

A US gallon is a unit of volume equal to 231 cubic inches or 3.78541 liters. A liter is a metric unit of volume equal to 1 cubic decimeter or 1,000 cubic centimeters. The Imperial gallon (used in the UK) is larger at 4.54609 liters.

History

The US gallon evolved from the English wine gallon, standardized in 1707 during Queen Anne's reign. The liter was introduced in France in 1795 as part of the metric system revolution. The US retained the Queen Anne wine gallon while the UK later adopted the Imperial gallon in 1824, creating the difference between US and Imperial measures that persists today.
