Milliliters to Cups Converter

Definition

A milliliter (ml) is a metric unit of volume equal to one-thousandth of a liter. A cup is a unit of volume used in cooking and serving, with varying definitions around the world. In the US, a cup equals 236.588 milliliters, while the metric cup used in Australia, Canada, and New Zealand equals 250 milliliters.

History

The milliliter was established as part of the metric system during the French Revolution in the late 18th century. It has remained consistent as 1/1000 of a liter. The cup as a measurement has ancient origins, but standardized cups emerged in the 19th and 20th centuries with the US cup becoming defined as 8 fluid ounces (236.588 ml) and the metric cup as 250 ml. The variation in cup sizes across cultures continues to present challenges in international recipe conversion.
