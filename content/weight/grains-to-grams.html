Grains to Grams Converter

Definition

A grain is a unit of mass equal to 0.06479891 grams. It is a traditionally used unit in many fields including firearms, archery, and pharmacy. A gram is the base unit of mass in the metric system, defined as one thousandth of a kilogram.

History

The grain was originally defined as the weight of a grain of wheat or barley, dating back to ancient times. It became standardized in England around the 13th century. The gram was introduced as part of the metric system during the French Revolution in the 1790s, defined based on the weight of a specific volume of water.
