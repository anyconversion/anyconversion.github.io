Pounds to Ounces Converter

Definition

A pound is a unit of weight in the imperial and US customary systems, equal to 16 ounces or exactly 0.45359237 kilograms. An ounce is a unit of weight in the imperial and US customary systems, equal to 1/16 of a pound or approximately 28.35 grams. One pound equals exactly 16 ounces.

History

The pound has ancient origins, deriving from the Roman "libra" (hence the abbreviation "lb"). The ounce derives from the Roman "uncia," which was 1/12 of a Roman pound. In medieval England, various pound weights were used for different commodities, but the avoirdupois system (which uses 16 ounces to a pound) became dominant for most goods by the 16th century. The current international definitions were standardized in 1959 by agreement between the United States and countries of the Commonwealth, defining the pound as exactly 0.45359237 kilograms and the ounce as 1/16 of a pound.
