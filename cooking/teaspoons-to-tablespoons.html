Teaspoons to Tablespoons Converter

Definition

A teaspoon (tsp) is a unit of volume used primarily in cooking and medicine, equal to 1/3 of a tablespoon or approximately 4.92892 milliliters in the US. A tablespoon (tbsp) is a larger unit of volume, equivalent to 3 teaspoons or about 14.78676 milliliters in the US. These measurements are part of the US customary system and vary slightly in other countries (e.g., an Australian tablespoon is 20 mL).

History

The teaspoon originated as a literal spoon used for tea, evolving into a standardized measure in the 17th century as recipes became more precise. The tablespoon followed as a larger counterpart, with its name reflecting its use for serving or eating from the table. Standardized in the US in the 19th century, these units became essential in cookbooks, reflecting the growing popularity of home cooking and baking.
