Exabytes to Zettabytes Converter

Definition

An exabyte (EB) is a unit of digital storage equal to 1,000 petabytes or 10¹⁸ bytes, used for vast data volumes like global internet traffic. A zettabyte (ZB) is 1,000 exabytes or 10²¹ bytes, representing colossal scales such as planetary data storage forecasts.

History

The exabyte was named in the late 1990s as data storage needs surged with the internet, using the prefix “exa” (10¹⁸). The zettabyte followed in the 2000s, coined from “zetta” (10²¹), as projections for global data growth—like the “zettabyte era” predicted around 2010—highlighted massive digital expansion.
