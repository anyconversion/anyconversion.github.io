Definition

A British Thermal Unit (BTU or Btu) is a unit of energy defined as the amount needed to raise 1 pound of water by 1Â°F, equal to approximately 1055.05585262 joules. A joule (J) is the SI unit of energy, equal to 1 newton-meter.

History

The BTU originated in Britain in the 19th century for steam and heating industries, standardized in the US by the early 20th century for HVAC applications. The joule, named after James Prescott Joule, was established in 1882 and adopted by the SI system in 1960, replacing older units like BTU.
