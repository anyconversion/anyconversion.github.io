Kilowatt-Hours to Joules Converter

Definition

A kilowatt-hour (kWh) is a unit of energy equal to 1,000 watts of power sustained for one hour, or 3,600,000 joules, commonly used for electrical energy billing. A joule (J) is the SI unit of energy, representing 1 newton-meter of work.

History

The kilowatt-hour was introduced in the late 19th century with the rise of electricity generation, standardized by utility companies for metering by the early 20th century. The joule, named after James Prescott Joule, was defined in 1882 and adopted by the SI system in 1960 for scientific use.
