History

Fuel efficiency units evolved with the automotive age: miles per gallon (MPG) emerged in the US in the early 20th century with car standardization, imperial MPG in the UK used a larger gallon, and metric units like kilometers per liter and liters per 100 kilometers gained traction in Europe and beyond post-WWII as global trade grew.
