MPG (Imperial) to MPG (US) Converter

Definition

Miles per gallon (MPG, Imperial) measures fuel efficiency using the imperial gallon, equal to 4.54609 liters, common in the UK and some Commonwealth countries. Miles per gallon (MPG, US) uses the US gallon, equal to 3.785411784 liters, standard in the United States.

History

MPG (Imperial) evolved in the UK in the early 20th century, based on the imperial gallon defined in 1824, reflecting British automotive standards. MPG (US) emerged around the same time in the US, using the smaller US gallon from the 1700s Queen Anne wine gallon, adapted for American vehicles and fuel economy reporting.
