BTU per Hour to Watts Converter

Definition

BTU per hour (BTU/h) is a unit of power measuring the rate of energy transfer, where 1 BTU (British Thermal Unit) is the energy to raise 1 pound of water by 1Â°F, and 1 BTU/h equals about 0.29307107 watts. A watt (W) is the SI unit of power, 1 joule per second.

History

The BTU originated in the 19th century in Britain for heating and cooling systems, standardized in the US for HVAC industries by the early 20th century. The watt, named after James Watt, was adopted in 1882 and formalized in 1960 by the SI system, replacing older units like BTU/h in science.
