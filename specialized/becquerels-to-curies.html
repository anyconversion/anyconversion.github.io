Becquerels to Curies Converter

Definition

A becquerel (Bq) is the SI unit of radioactivity, measuring one nuclear decay per second. A curie (Ci) is an older unit of radioactivity, equal to 3.7 × 10¹⁰ decays per second, based on the activity of 1 gram of radium-226.

History

The becquerel was adopted in 1975 by the General Conference on Weights and Measures, named after Henri Becquerel, who discovered radioactivity. The curie was defined in 1910 by Marie and Pierre Curie, honoring their work with radium, and was widely used before the SI system standardized the becquerel.
