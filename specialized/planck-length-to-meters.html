Planck Length to Meters Converter

Definition

The Planck length is a fundamental unit of length in physics, approximately 1.616255 × 10⁻³⁵ meters, derived from constants like the speed of light, gravitational constant, and Planck’s constant. A meter (m) is the base unit of length in the metric system, defined as the distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 seconds.

History

Introduced by Max Planck in 1899, the Planck length represents a scale where quantum gravity effects become significant, marking a theoretical limit of measurable distance. The meter was established in 1791 during the French Revolution, initially as 1/10,000,000 of the Earth’s meridian quadrant, and refined with modern definitions in the 20th century.
